Part of this topic has to do with energy exchange between beings in the physical realm, energy exchange and how that is balanced. For many people, part of their development, part of their growth, is involved experiencing energy exchange. There’s a template between a man and a woman in terms of energy that exists for eons. The template goes something like this:

 

The woman nurtures the man through intimacy, and the man supports the house. Then they have children and the man supports the children and the family and the woman maintains the house. That is the template; although in today’s world it’s not operating that well. I’m sure some people are still working it and it’s working; it gives people a reason to be together. It gives them a purpose: to bring new children into the world. Of course, how does it look physiologically between a man and a woman?

 

I’m going to be talking about men and women. Of course, I’m more of an expert at men than women, but I’ll try to say how I think it works. I probably had lifetimes as a woman at one point or another, so there’s some kind of cellular memory there.

 

A man has a physiological need to release. When I say this, I’m not talking about all men; I’m talking about the standard template. In specific, there can be lots and lots of variations. If you don’t fit this description, it’s okay. But in general, men have an intense physiological need to release. If they don’t do it, whether they do it by themselves or with someone else, if they don’t do it they get dysfunctional; they get headaches; they get pains in their shoulders; they feel unworthy, for some. Again, not everyone, but this is the template.

 

The woman: the template is nurturing. So you get this man that has this need for release and you get this woman who has her normal function as being nurturing and they come together. They both have something that the other needs. When they come together, there is usually some kind of unconscious, unspoken contract. It’s not usually said; because it’s not spoken both people don’t understand the terms of their agreement.

 

So they come together and they don’t really realize they’re making agreements at that point. The come together and they have a connection. The man feels good and the woman is nurturing. But then the man is off and the woman is still nurturing. Oftentimes the woman feels empty. She feels like she gave nurturing to this man and he’s disappeared. She really expects that even though it’s not an explicit agreement, the man is going to be giving back. He is going to be spending money on her. He’s going to be taking her out. He’s going to be marrying her, in the older model and they’re going to bring up a family.

 

But, at least she expects something in return for nurturing the guy. If the guy does not realize that’s the case, then at some point it’s like he’s not honoring the unspoken contract. At some point you end up with the situation where the woman feels completely hurt and abandoned, because this guy isn’t giving back. She’s given her nurturing to him and he’s not giving back. He’s off with someone else, or he’s just gone wherever he’s gone and there’s hurt.

 

In that situation I can say that when people are younger they don’t realize the implications of these energy exchanges. It comes to the level of experience; whether they’re promiscuous or whether they have a girlfriend, some people intuitively honor these expectations, even though they don’t think about it consciously; and others don’t. Then it becomes a game of manipulation; this is where it goes really bad, where the woman is holding back her ability to nurture because the man [is not giving to her] and the man’s nurture is slowly falling apart.

 

I think most of you can relate to what I’m talking about. I’m using a man and a woman. It doesn’t have to be a man and a woman, because it could be two men or two women. If they’re having a real relationship, usually one of them has the opposite. In fact the Elohim have mentioned this about gay relationships where many times people had lots of lifetimes as a man, then they reincarnate as a woman, but they still have male energy and vice-versa.

 

The problem is they’re in a certain body but their energy-body is still the energy of the opposite sex. There becomes still, the same dynamic usually. Of course, again I’m talking about generalities. The important thing here is, even if you don’t fit into the generality, which many of you don’t, particularly in this group, are you bound? What is the contract? What is the level of expectation of the person you’re with? And what is your level of expectation with them? Are you fulfilling their level of expectation? Because if you’re not, then things are going to go south and it’s going to end up as one of those relationships that has resentment. People don’t talk to each other after it’s over. You never made a friendship with the person; you were using them. They were using you, because they wanted security. You wanted sex. When it comes to a close you never were friends.

 

Now, what is a relationship in Grace? This is not so easy. By the way, the distinctions I’m making don’t just work for intimate relationships; they can very easily work for friendships. People think, “As a friend I’ll be there for them; I am there for them. I’m calling them all the time. How come they’re never calling me? I want something in return. They’re not giving it to me.”

 

Sometimes you have someone who you think is your friend and then you have a really deep need of some sort. You call them and they’re not there for you. Sometimes people hate to call their friends when they have really deep need, because if that person lets them down at that moment, they probably will not consider them a friend anymore. They’ll be hurt. So, sometimes people don’t ask for things when they really need them. It’s complicated, because there’s not a rule about this. But, the point is: what are the contracts? What are the unconscious contracts people make with each other?

 

I remember my parents. My father always used to talk about, “He owes me a favor.” Obviously that’s not Grace. As soon as you’re talking about, thinking about “he owes me a favor”, it’s like you’re stacking up things you’re doing for someone else.

 

In your mind, you have this idea of an obligation that one day they’re going to give you back something. They might, because people operate that way. If they don’t, you’re going to feel screwed. How do we try to lift some of these energies out of this give-and-take karma into Grace? It can be done, but you’ve got to be brutally honest with how it is.

 

It’s okay if you have a karmic relationship. If you have one where there’s a level of expectation on both sides, make sure that you’re perfectly fulfilling the levels of obligation. Even though it’s not a Grace relationship, it cancels itself out if everything is being fulfilled. Very few people have a Grace relationship with their landlord. But if you fulfill the obligation, you’re paying the rent every month, then it cancels out and you have a place to live.

 

There are lots of places in your life where you have to have relationships of obligation; you have to in this realm. If you’re aiming for spiritual connection you have to be able to have some relationships of Grace, because that will keep your spirits lifted.

When people have been touched we get some of the most beautiful emails. I better not say their name, but somebody said, “You have become my family. That doesn’t mean we can’t have a $300 weekend if we choose to, because to do that it creates the opportunity for people to come together physically to meet each other. If you can’t afford it, you can still have the free stuff we do on the conference calls.

There has to be enough people to come that we charge enough for it so it covers the expenses. But people are still meeting each other on the line.

 

Going back to relationships and Grace:

 

The key is to make everything so balanced that there’s no residual. If you want to take it a step further, we are all needy in our own ways. Men are needy in their ways and women are needy in their ways. If you’re really into loving someone, you fulfill their need out of free-giving and care; both ways, in which case the relationship then has gratitude in it instead of obligation.

 

Those two things are both flip sides of the coin: obligation is the give-and-take in 3D: I did this for you, you did this for me, I gave you this and you gave me that. If you think about how gratitude feels, there’s no keeping count; you just feel it and it’s a Divine quality, gratitude. It’s different than appreciation and it’s different than acknowledgement. It’s an inner quality that just wells up inside of you that you feel.

 

I’m grateful that you guys come onto this call and I’m grateful that we have touched you. I’m grateful that there are people all over the world, a small group whose lives are changing as a result of what we’re doing. I’m grateful that some of you have picked up on this energy and are changing.

 

The key is: can you move one of these obligatory relationships into gratitude? Sometimes you can’t; sometimes you just have to keep it balanced by fulfilling the other person’s expectations and them fulfilling your expectations.

 

In relationships between a man and a woman there is something else that’s taking place. Again I say a man and a woman; I mean with two people that are involved with each other there’s an energy exchange. Every time we have an energy exchange we attract people that are different than ourselves and that have different holograms than we do. If two people have very close holograms, they have a hard time polarizing and having energy exchange back and forth. We need someone that’s different.

 

What happens is; as we connect with that person, we are educating our hologram. In the way they balance us there is whatever physical thing happens, but then there’s the energetic thing that happens. Their energies are opposite or different than ours and when they merge with ours, which is what happens in intimacy, we get an experience of a certain kind of balance inside of ourselves.

 

Of course, the first time you experience that it’s called falling in love and you just want that person and you want to be around them. Usually, it doesn’t last, because it goes into obligation and expectation after the romance is over.

 

If you can manage to keep that energy going then you’re never going to leave them anyway, because the energy is going, but the energy is teaching you how to expand your hologram; the duality of yin and yang. You’re learning about it. Just like when we’re on these calls and we experience this great loving energy from the other side and from each other we are learning how to expand our holograms. In this case, we’re doing it without necessarily needing another person to balance us.

 

As you learn that you integrate it and you start operating, integrating the two sides of yourself, the male and the female and you become complete. You lose your sexual identity and the need for someone else becomes less. But at a certain point in your life, it’s hard to deny that need, it’s so overwhelming. You have not learned how to integrate it, so you have to experience it with another person.

The higher thing is to do the right thing, the good thing, the noble thing; the thing that when you look back you’ll say to yourself, “I’m glad I did that; it makes me feel good in my heart.” That choice always comes back to us.

 

When you talked about you have these biological urges and drives I certainly understand that and I also know as an artist and as an at-one-time pretty serious athlete, that self-discipline and restraint are really critical. There is virtue; there is virtue.

Oftentimes, we’re faced with situations where people are pressuring us or they’re bullying us. Support people that are coming from their hearts.
